South Asian cities have always negotiated water, heat, and dense movement. Older neighborhoods often encoded climate wisdom that modern concrete expansion ignored.

## Learning from the Past

Traditional courtyards, raised walkways, and shaded street edges were not decorative choices. They were survival design for extreme rain and humidity.

### Practical Modern Adaptations

- Permeable pavements to reduce flash flooding
- Elevated electrical systems in risk zones
- Mixed-use shelter hubs for emergency response

Future planning can be both modern and rooted in local memory.
