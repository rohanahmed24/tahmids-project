Cities are learning to grow food closer to where people live. Rooftop farms, indoor hydroponics, and neighborhood gardens are becoming practical responses to climate pressure and supply chain fragility.

## Why This Trend Matters

Urban agriculture is not only about vegetables. It also improves community resilience, reduces transport emissions, and creates local jobs. In dense cities, every square meter has to do more work.

### What Is Changing Fast

- Building codes now include green roof incentives
- Grocery chains test direct sourcing from vertical farms
- Schools use garden programs to teach nutrition and ecology

The next decade of food policy may be designed as much by mayors and architects as by traditional farming ministries.
